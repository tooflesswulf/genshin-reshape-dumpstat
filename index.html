Genshin artifact reshape math

Artifact reshaping & dump stats.

Artifact reshaping lets you guarantee 2 substat rolls. The unintuitive part: if you only keep well-rolled pieces, spending one of those picks on a stat you don't want can yield better results.

A calculator is available at the Genshin Optimizer under the tab (Artifact Upgrader → Reshape). Requires the Irminsul scanner for artifact initial value data.

01

Reshape guarantees

Each upgrade roll lands on one of the artifact's 4 substats at random. Reshaping lets you pick 2 (resp. 3 or 4)* substats, and the game promises at least 2 rolls will land on your picks. The game fulfills that promise by landing the last few rolls on your picks if the first few go poorly, like a pity system. However, this also means that only outcomes with exactly 2 picked rolls are boosted, and the rest are unchanged.

02

Consequence of reshape guarantees

The main statistical consequence is that only outcomes with exactly 2 picked rolls are boosted. The figure below shows the chance of each outcome, sorted in increasing damage* order.

While reshaping, the game allows you to revert the outcome if you don't like it. This keep-or-revert threshold manifests in the chart as a vertical line where everything to the left is ignored, since those outcomes would be reverted anyways. So when you're pickier about what you keep, the boosts to ( (1 CRIT + 1 DEF) and (2 CRIT) ) are what cement (CRIT & DEF) as the unintuitive, but optimal choice.

So what do you pick?

Use the Genshin Optimizer to optimize your artifact reshaping decisions. The best pairs can vary, and sometimes you don't even pick the best stat. Reshaping with 3 or 4 guarantees also exhibits the same unintuitive behavior. In this example, the gap between the best choice vs the obvious pick can be as large as 20%.
